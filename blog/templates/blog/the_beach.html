The Beach

when i imagine the tiny homer,

this man is small.


crepuscular, and in his most solid form

and a fossilised relic,

i must think back to the ancients:

aristotle, jesus, even to god himself.

whose wisdom surely could buttress my feebling

attempts to climb this dank pier

from what i am able to see,

i the mind's eye of my own puerile and mortal insignificance,

my ignorance only reinforces my comparatively weak and mollusc-like form.


when i die,

the world sighs its collective relief

and me, finally, released:

i see a cornucopia,

homer forever reclining,

endlessly consuming his food items,

to maintain the eternal melody of spherical apprehension

whom'st curved surface is but a microcosm of
